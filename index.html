Research

Research in the lab focuses on the large-scale (phylogenetic) mechanisms and implications of speciation, the process by which entirely new living species are born. Rather than crossing or genetically manipulating small plants and animals, our team learns from broad evolutionary patterns — in genes and traits — across species, in both time and space.

Our approach is interdisciplinary: We take our questions, hypotheses and data from biology, and drawing from statistics and computer science we then create the necessary software for analytical work. Although scripting and programming is our bread and butter, we believe useful computational biology must be grounded in deep biological scholarship and built on close partnerships with empiricists.
