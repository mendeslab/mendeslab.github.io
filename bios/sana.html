Sana Raza (PhD student, 1st year)

Short Bio: Hello! I am Sana. I am from the city of Karachi, Pakistan, located just on the coastline of the Arabian Sea. Despite fish being one of the popular dishes there because of the location, for unknown reasons, I somehow have always strongly disliked the taste of it (and continue to, to this day). Coming from a family where most of the people were engineers, I had to pick between becoming an engineer and a doctor. I chose engineering because that meant I did not have to study medicine, which I did not have an interest in. Some of my engineering years were most amazing because I was not learning just one subject like maths but an interesting combination of different subjects like numerical methods for biomedical engineers. I realized during that time that interdisciplinary subjects are something that I love exploring! That is also one of the reasons why I chose computational evolutionary biology as a topic, as it sits at the intersection of statistics, programming, and evolution — the latter subject being one I accidentally discovered by reading Jonathan Weiner's "The Beak of the Finch".
