Clementine Yan (PhD student, 1st year)

Short Bio: Hi, this is Clementine / 严杏汝 (Xingru). I was born and raised in the biggest city in southwestern China, Chengdu, and brought up by my grandparents and my dad, who is a biologist focusing on plant resources, microenvironments, and pharmacology. When I was 15, I moved to New Zealand to live with my mom, who works as an editor for a medical publishing company. My high school was next to Orewa Beach, which is one of the best beaches on the North Shore, so besides studying, I also spent a lot of time enjoying the sunshine, the beach, and all the beautiful views given by the god of nature. With the expectations of a family full of doctors, I went to the University of Auckland to study Biomedical Science and soon realised that anatomy and physiology were not really my thing. Instead, I discovered a stronger interest in biology, statistics, and computation, which led me to pursue a master's degree studying protein structural evolution. After spending a decade in New Zealand, I thought it was time to move continents again. So here I am in the U.S., doing my PhD in Dr. Mendes' lab at LSU, and getting ready to start a new adventure.
